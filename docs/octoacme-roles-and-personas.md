# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads oversee quality assurance activities, develop test strategies, and validate that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards before release. They coordinate testing efforts across manual and automated approaches.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain test plans aligned with project scope and acceptance criteria
- Coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Verify features meet acceptance criteria and Definition of Done
- Identify and communicate quality risks and defects
- Collaborate with developers on test automation and coverage
- Conduct final release validation and sign-off

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal production defects
- Improve test coverage and automation efficiency
- Reduce time to validate features while maintaining quality standards

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report on testing progress and blockers
- PR reviews for testability and quality checks
- Weekly delivery syncs to flag quality risks
- Pre-release sign-off meetings
- Input to risk registers and release notes

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate deployment activities, manage release windows, and ensure smooth transitions from development to production. They prepare rollback plans and coordinate releases across product teams.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule deployment windows
- Coordinate cutover activities and release logistics
- Prepare and validate rollback and mitigation plans
- Manage release communications across teams
- Verify deployment readiness and post-deploy validation
- Maintain release documentation and runbooks

### Goals
- Execute zero-downtime deployments when possible
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and customer impact
- Ensure clear communication and coordination during releases

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release planning meetings with PM, developers, and support
- Deployment window coordination and notifications
- Post-deployment status updates and verifications
- Release notes and stakeholder announcements
- Incident response coordination if rollback is needed

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user interfaces and workflows that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with customer needs. They gather user feedback and validate design decisions through research and usability testing.

### Responsibilities
- Design user interfaces, interactions, and workflows
- Conduct user research and validate design through testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Ensure accessibility standards and responsive design
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria and usability requirements
- Gather and incorporate user feedback iteratively

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered experiences
- Improve usability metrics and customer satisfaction
- Ensure consistent design language and accessibility compliance

### Typical Communication
- Early planning sessions with Product Manager to understand requirements
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with developers
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Contribution to acceptance criteria and feature specs
- Participation in demos and retrospectives

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders represent business and user interests, providing strategic inputs, requirements, and approvals throughout the project lifecycle. They review deliverables and ensure alignment with organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and strategic context
- Review and approve project charters and milestones
- Represent customer or business unit needs
- Participate in key decision points and trade-off discussions
- Review project deliverables and provide feedback
- Champion the project within the organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Maintain alignment with organizational priorities
- Support resource allocation and remove organizational blockers

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation meetings and charter reviews
- Monthly stakeholder update briefings
- Major milestone reviews and demo sessions
- Approval gates for scope changes or releases
- Escalation discussions for business-impacting decisions

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholders and technical teams by capturing requirements, mapping business processes, and ensuring alignment between business needs and technical solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements
- Map and analyze business processes and workflows
- Identify data requirements and reporting needs
- Support prioritization and trade-off analysis
- Validate that solutions address business problems
- Create process documentation and user stories

### Goals
- Ensure clear translation of business needs into actionable requirements
- Identify process improvements and optimization opportunities
- Reduce requirement gaps and rework

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Collaboration with Product Manager on backlog refinement
- Work with developers to clarify requirements and edge cases
- Contribution to project one-pagers and acceptance criteria
- Participation in planning, demos, and retrospectives
